    A FRACTION OF JUSTICE

    Chapter #6: Up in Smoke

    Word Count: 3,053
    Read Time: Approx. 27 mins

    [Alexander’s POV]

    My senses came flooding back all at once. I was lying flat on some hard surface. Unable to hear anything but a horrible high-pitched ring that rattled my brain, I took a shaky breath. I felt my right leg being pushed and prodded, I kicked in a weak protest against what I assumed were children’s torturous fingers looking to tear me apart. My eyes were assaulted with a painful white light that set the inside of my eyelids ablaze. I furrowed my brow as I registered the searing pain in the back of my skull. Was it fractured? Jake must’ve whacked me with the dragon sculpture to prevent my escape. The throbbing in my cranium and neck seemed to support this hypothesis. I went to touch the crown of my head and feel for blood, but when I flexed the muscles of my left arm, something was holding it in place. My heart started to thunder at a tremendous pace, as I snapped open my eyes. 

    Oh no. 

    I was lying belly up on the desk, that too familiar surface where I had whiled away the majority of my lifespan on paperwork. The gold and green table lamp I’d clamored up the night before, was now trained directly over me, searing, hot, electrical light pouring down like the unobstructed sun in a desert. It blinded me, and I blinked rapidly to regain my vision. I couldn’t see anything beyond the light, and my hearing was still muffled at best. I felt blind, trapped and completely helpless. I pulled hard against whatever was holding my left wrist down. I felt the tearing of fabric as I wrenched myself free. 

    “Uh oh, Aiden! He’s escaping!” I heard that clearly enough, it was practically screamed at top volume directly into my sensitive ears. 

    “Hold him down! I’ll be right there!” 

    Straining my eyes, trying to make sense of the shadows that seemed to loom above me, getting ever closer, I shouted in protest, “No, no! Wait—“ I was silenced by the flat of a palm pressing down over my chest, neck and head. I thrashed wildly, despite the pain in my skull. Shrouded in darkness, slivers of light occasionally peeking through stubby, child fingers, I struggled to breathe. I felt my wrist squeezed between a thumb and index, thrown down to my side. I was completely overpowered. I shouted and hissed through my prison of living flesh. 

    I felt something slam down immediately next to my arm. Fear consuming my every nerve, I froze, my chest heaving as blood pounded in my ears and neck. 

    “You’re not going anywhere! Jake, move your hand.” The humid darkness melted away and I gratefully sucked in a lungful of fresh air, only to be greeted with a terrifying vision: Aiden’s cobalt blue eyes gleaming with wicked delight. His lips twisted into a smile as he held something aloft between his thumb and forefinger: a pushpin. 

    For the first time in my life I was at a loss for words. The razor sharp object was no bigger than his thumbnail, but was at least as long as the space from the tips of my fingers to the base of my wrist. With a few pounds of pressure, he could stab me in any of my vital organs and kill me, easily. I trembled from head to toe, realizing my arm was pinned to the surface of the desk by another one of these dangerous spikes. He was right, there was nowhere for me to go. “Aiden… Listen… I, I was just joking with you earlier. That was all, just, just a funny joke. I, I wasn’t running away just… playing tag and… and I wanted a head start…” 

    “You shouldn’t lie… It’s bad to lie. Especially to big kids like us, who can hurt you…” He cast a glance at the pin in his fingers. I swallowed hard, holding up my free arm defensively, I exploded with language to try to fix this mess I was in. Logic was my weapon and who could go wrong with that? 

    “C-C’mon, Aiden… Let’s just take a deep breath, yeah?” I mimicked deep breathing, which helped to keep my own racing heart from bursting through my chest, “In and out… Yeah, you’ve got it. I can see you’ve got some really big feelings you’re struggling with… Why don’t you just put that down and we can t-talk this through, huh? You don’t really want to hurt me. I’m just an, unfortunately, very vulnerable target for your outward expressions of anger. You’re embarrassed because I made you feel stupid in front of your brother. And that makes you feel small, doesn’t it? Believe me, I know the feeling. It’s hard to admit you’ve been outwitted or overshadowed, especially by someone you consider to be beneath you… It makes sense you just want to throttle the life out of something completely helpless just to feel powerful again. I get it. You know, aggression is often a symptom of misplaced grief…”

    “Ugh! Shut up!” 

    He wasn’t getting it. Of course not. “Well, Aiden, I’m just elucidating… Sorry, in simpler terms, that means I’m revealing or explaining, some truths that I think are very important for a young man such as yourself to start to understand.” As much as you’re capable of understanding anything. “I bet your dad doesn’t give you the attention and approval you desperately crave at this stage in your cognitive development, huh? He’s pretty busy most of the time isn’t he? And then, you’ve always got Jake riding your coattails, needing support and reassurance from you. You feel trapped. You know, in psychology that phenomenon is called parentification, have you heard of that before?” No? Of course not. “Well, it can be very damaging for young minds. Have you ever stopped to think this might be the root of your problem? I think we can both agree, killing me is not going to magically fix your broken relationship with your father, right? You can at least understand that?”

    “Stop saying mean things about my dad! You’re weird and… and you talk too much!” So I’ve been told by two generations before you, countless times. I could see fat tear droplets welling in the corners of his eyes. Alright, we were finally getting somewhere. Maybe I was in the clear. 

    “Aww, I know. It’s hard to… feel things…” I tried to sound empathetic, I wasn’t sure it worked. I’m certain it’s rather challenging for your underdeveloped amygdala to experience anything but blind rage, you dull, simple creature. “Well, why don’t you just unpin me here and we can get back to having, ideally non-fatal, fun? How’s that sound?” I flashed him a winning smile. 

    “No.” Damn you.

    “Wh-what do you mean no? Didn’t we just have a nice bonding moment there?” 

    “Stop telling me what to do!” A finger flew towards me, colliding with my chest and slamming me against the hard floor of the desk. The air was crushed out of my lungs. 

    “Woah, woah, Aiden. Aiden, buddy…” That’s how to relate to kids, right? “Let’s just calm down… deep breaths, remember?” 

    “Just… Just stop talking like that!! You’re the little one, not me! You can’t talk to me like I’m just a kid! You belong to me!” Well, I believe every man belongs principally to himself, but agree to disagree. “You have to do whatever I want cuz I’m like a hundred times bigger than you! So you don’t get free until I say!” Well, that’s not very nice. 

    “See, see, this is what I’m trying to get you to understand. You’re projecting these power dynamics onto me, when really you should be focusing on self-differentiation from your emotionally abusive parent—“

    “I SAID STOP!”

    Uh-oh. It would seem I did not handle this as well as I’d thought. What did I say wrong? Every word was the truth! 

    Suddenly his fist, armed with a pushpin came hurtling toward me. Like an animal caught in a trap (which, I suppose, they thought me to be) I trembled, trying my best to squirm away from the blows despite being rooted to the ground. His first attempt landed just shy of my right shoulder, buried in the wood. I wouldn’t be able to out maneuver him forever, this was clearly a losing battle. Blood pounded in my battered skull as I twisted away from the deadly point. He missed again, this time brushing against my left heel. That wicked smile came back, as he relished in my fear. He dug the pin out of the wood for the third time, wielding it high above me and then cascading it down with deadly purpose. I kicked and writhed but as it got closer and closer, the truth was undeniable: he was going to hit his mark this time. At the final second, I squeezed my eyes shut, holding my breath for the searing pain of metal tearing through flesh and muscle. I felt pressure baring down on my right calf… and then, nothing…

    “You got him!” Jake shouted triumphantly, “Wait… why isn’t he screaming?” 

    Slowly, I opened my eyes, spying the crimson red plastic rising up out of my leg. I didn’t feel a thing. My case files! The deadly point had buried itself in its pages. I heaved a sigh of relief. 

    Wait… my case files!

     I lurched forward just as Aiden’s fingers wrapped around my leg. “No, no, no, no! Don’t!” I wrestled with desperate hands against his forceful grip, but of course, he could have snapped my wrist in two if he’d felt so inclined. There was nothing I could do to stop him, still I protested.

    “What’re you hiding? I told you, no lying. I’m gonna punish you for lying! Lemme see…”

    He threw my limb about as if it were as weightless as a feather. Prying and pulling up on my slacks to find the hidden pages, both boys gasped when they found what they were looking for. 

    Jake, as was apparently characteristic, sucked the air from the room again, “He’s hiding some papers! Oh! Do you think it’s a treasure map?? OOH! Or maybe it tells us how to find the magic?!”

    “He’s not magic, Jake. He lied to us to try to run away. Whatever it is, you really want it, though, don’t you?” He addressed the question to me. I saw an awful idea formulate in the child’s mind. Against my will, I cowered. With another heart-stopping flurry of giant motion, he pulled the pin from my leg, then gripping me by the knee so I couldn’t move, he raised the sharp point in the air. I fought and spat, but between being pinned to the desk and held captive in between two excruciating fingers, I couldn’t move an inch.  

    “Wait, wait, please!!” With one fluid motion, he dragged the razor sharp edge along the underside of my calf, tearing my slacks, ripping into my flesh and slicing the ribbon that held my entire life’s work, in one fell swoop. I screamed in pain as blood oozed up from the cut and pages cascaded to the floor. Because I was still rooted by my left sleeve, no matter how much I wrenched and writhed, I couldn’t reach the pile of papers. He snatched all of it from me, pinching twenty years worth of painstaking, meticulous research between his dirty finger and thumb. “Aiden, look, I didn’t mean to offend you… Please give those back. Please. They’re extremely important. Not just to me, but a whole lot of people.” I swallowed my pride, as much as it boiled my blood, “I… I’m sorry. Okay? Is that what you want? An apology? Well, here it is. I’m sorry. Are you satisfied now with your little power game? Just give it back.” I reached out my hand, half afraid he’d plunge the pin into the center of my palm, instead. 

    “Okay.” Wait, what? I blinked, wholly taken aback. Without waiting for my response, he pulled the pin out that held my arm, before pinching my collar between two fingers and plucking me up off the desk. I cried out in shock at being suspended again, I’d gone so long with my feet on solid ground! He carried me back to the rug, before the fire that was still burning from when they’d tortured me, earlier. Jake scampered along, excited to see what new game his brother had in store for their shiny new toy (alternatively known as, me). 

    I didn’t trust him one bit. I knew he had some wicked plan concocted in his head. He set me down on my own two feet, while he sat on his knees to my right, Jake, lying down with his head propped up in his hands, stared at me from the left. I swallowed as I plucked my collar away from my windpipe. He still held my case between two fingers. 

    “You want this?” 

    “Well… y-yes,”

    “Then come get it.” Oh no. Not this. Don’t do this to me. My shoulders drooped. What other choice did I have? 

    I hardly noticed my bleeding calf as I lunged forward, arms outstretched to reclaim my prize. He allowed me to get as far as brushing my fingertips against those familiar pages, before flicking me in the face with his free hand and pulling the pages just out of my reach. I cried out and stumbled, as blood poured from my nose. The boys burst into laughter. 

    “I wanna try!” Aiden passed my work on to his brother. Even he, as stupid as he was, could outpace me with no effort at all. Every time I got within arm’s length, he’d move impossibly far and I’d sprint to catch up. This was all absolutely delightful for him and he couldn’t keep himself from exploding with laughter, each time I failed. My lungs burning in my chest, and my heart thrumming as sweat poured from my brow, I admit that tears of hopelessness welled up and blurred my vision. My whole life’s purpose, everything I had, was being ripped away from me by snotty-nosed brats. 

    “Come on, you want this, right?” Aiden shook the leaf of papers, teasingly, in my face as I wiped the blood and sweat out of my eyes. “All you have to do is come get it, it’s right here!”

    I lunged forward, knowing it was useless but being unable to stop myself. I got closer and closer to his offered hand. I was right up on it now… within arm’s reach… my fingers brushed the paper… they closed around it… I tightened my grip. Was he actually going to let me have them? Had he gotten all the torture out of it he wanted? I felt lighter than air… and then, suddenly, I was. In an instant, I was held aloft again, this time whipping through the room with mind-bending speed.

    I felt the heat before I recognized the threat. He was throwing me into the fire! There was no other explanation. I was a dead man, after all! I screamed as the flames got inextricably close. I shut my eyes. But all that came was the sound of giant laughter, echoing painfully in my ear canals. Reopening my eyes, I immediately saw the source of their amusement. 

    He’d held me to the flame, not to slaughter me, no, to shatter my soul instead. The papers I had finally earned back were now ablaze, curling into black smoke as their ember-laced edges faded into oblivion. 

    I’d have to let them go if I wanted to keep from roasting alive. He hadn’t just taken my work away from me, he’d made me destroy it myself. As the flames licked and devoured more and more, I pulled my hands closer to the safe edge. Eventually, that wasn’t enough, as flames continued to ravage the only thing I’d ever truly owned in my time on this earth. I let it go, watching the rest crumble to ash and smoke. 

    He wrapped a fist around my body, pulling me before his gaze, his face aglow, his smile cocksure, “What’s wrong? I thought you wanted it.” 

    I couldn’t help it. Tears spilled over and stained my cheeks as I watched the smoke wisp and curl away. “Are you, are you crying??” They laughed, delighted. “What a little scardey-cat! Did we destroy your diary? I told you, if you were bad, I’d make you pay!” He grabbed my face between the pads of two fingers, squeezing my cheeks. 

    A molten hot rage ignited inside of me. Animalistic, feverish and unthinking, I did the only thing I could: I bit him. Hard. 

    My teeth sunk into the soft flesh between his finger and thumb. As soon as I felt hot blood gush into my mouth, he wailed in pain. He threw me to the ground as he screamed, instantly sobbing. I fell in a crumpled heap, but had at least managed to land on the rug. 

    “DAD!!! DAD!!!!!! HE BIT ME!!! IT HUUUURTS!!” Oh, shut up you big baby! The noise was excruciating, I didn’t know humans could even reach that high of a pitch and that resonant of a volume at the same time. I tried desperately to untangle myself, while throwing my hands over my ears.

    Their father burst into the room, much to my surprise almost exactly in the same disgruntled manner as his father before him, “Boys! What is going on in here? Why all the screaming? I’m on a call! What is the deal?” 

    Not dialing it down in any way, his face splotchy and tear stained, the eldest son was practically sucking his own thumb, screaming, “DADDY!! HE BIT MY HAND! LOOK!” 

    The man clenched his jaw, before sighing,“I-I’m so sorry, sir. I am so sorry, I am going to have to call you back… It seems my son has gotten himself hurt.” Whatever the call, it was clearly important, and he was not happy about it being interrupted. He knelt down (with significant effort, thanks to bad knees and poor posture) and examined the boy’s hand. Why I didn’t take this opportunity to run, I don’t know. I think I was in shock. 

    Instead I just sat there, where I’d been thrown on the rug, watching this unfold, knowing I had reached the end of my life. Almost no pet bites a human and lives. It was actually a codified federal law, just like for dogs, that the injured party could use against the owner in court, if necessary. This man was legally within his rights to execute me now, despite the fact that I was certain the wound I inflicted would disappear within a week. 

    Suddenly, I was broken from my reverie as I felt three pairs of cruel eyes staring at me. Now I bolted, unable to withstand the murder in their gaze. I barely got to the edge of the rug before finding myself engulfed in a huge, hairy fist. I yelped as my feet left the floor. His face was twisted with rage. He squeezed my body until I turned red, the vein in my neck bulging, while I hacked for air, “You little fucker!!!” He screamed in my face, spit flying into my hair and down my cheek, I cringed, “You bit my son? What the fuck for?? Huh?” Did he want the honest answer: that his son was a little psychopath? “Answer me!”

    “Y-Yes! But…. But I thought I was going to get thrown into the fire, I-I’m sorr—” He flicked me on the bridge of the nose, dulling all of my senses and making my face gush with blood again. I couldn’t free my hands to shield my broken face, instead, I shouted, exasperated, as I spit out the crimson liquid, trying uselessly to squirm free of his iron grip. His hands crushed around my body, leaving only my head and neck up to my collarbone exposed, my arms pinned to my sides, my legs dangling awkwardly from the knee down, outside of his fist.

    “That’s it! Boys, sorry, but playtime is over.” The two children stared up at their father with giddy anticipation. How would he make me pay for my sins? His booming voice interrupted my thoughts, “I should’ve just thrown you out with the trash when I had the chance.” I winced as his fingers squeezed ever tighter around me. He knit his brow determinedly, “C’mon, let’s go outside.” I couldn’t believe I was about to lose my life to the stupidest, most pathetic man-child I’d ever had the misfortune to know. The old man had been a nightmare in his own right, but this, this was hell itself.
