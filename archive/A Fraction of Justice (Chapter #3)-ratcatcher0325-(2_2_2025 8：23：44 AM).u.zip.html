It’s time for him to get ready to leave the only home he’s known for most of his life! Previous: Chapter #2Next: Chapter #4CW: Dehumanization, mild abuse________________________________________A FRACTION OF JUSTICEChapter #3: PreparationsWord Count: 1,108Read Time: Approx. 10 mins[Alexander’s POV]I awoke in the early afternoon to blissful quiet. I rolled over to face the room, nothing but soft, bright light filtering through the windows, the piles of paper undisturbed on the desk below. I liked where my bed was positioned, on the fifth shelf just shy of the center of the room. The thing I liked most was that it was about eye level with most humans. So as I lay there, comfortable and warm with no one forcibly dragging me from my bed, I could see the world from a human perspective. The thought always made my heart race a bit. To be that big. It was difficult to fathom even though I thought about it almost every day. Pushing up to sitting, I let my legs swing over the shelf, just drinking in the solitude of the moment. The more my mind cleared from the fog of sleep, I began to calculate my next steps. I needed to gather as many supplies as I could; non perishable food and as much water as I could carry, being the most important. Excited to solve a brand new problem, I rose to standing and got dressed (in a full suit, of course), before clamoring down to the floor. I noticed with satisfaction that my hand stung less. I was lucky, the glass hadn’t cut too deep. I proceeded to walk to the kitchen, a bit of a journey for me. It had been at least a few years since I’d left the four walls of the library for any other part of the house. Hands in pockets, I gazed at the monumental furniture, as antique as the man who’d owned it, ornate, styled, imposing. I whiled away a few hours clamoring up and down dangerous pantry shelves, trying to gather as much as I could. I didn’t mind helping myself to fistfuls of crackers, chips, and prepackaged cakes while I was at it. The healthiest of choices? Absolutely not. But they were edible straight out of the bag  and within my admittedly limited reach. I built a bag of sorts out of a strip of burlap potato sack and the most haphazard sewing job ever known to man or pet. I’d never touched a needle before, but understood enough about the basic mechanics to make it work… sort of. I’d been lucky to find a sewing kit within reach inside the old man’s massive walk in closet. Before the arthritis had taken over, he’d been known to make minor repairs and alterations to his own wardrobe. With the fruits of my labor slung over my shoulder, filled to the brim with various snack foods and a small plastic container with a screw on lid that had once held food dye, filled with clean water, I found myself back in the oh so familiar library. I drummed my fingers against my thigh. I needed to prioritize getting out of here. But… the library was all mine now. I could finally satisfy my curiosity about anything I was interested in. I was practically salivating. A quick browse couldn’t hurt, right? I figured I had at least twenty four hours before anyone would arrive. Satisfied, I first clamored up the desk. Remembering I’d just irresponsibly left my work splayed out, exposed, ready for anyone to blow into chaos and disarray with nothing more than a puff of air from their giant lungs. I gathered my papers and strapped them back against my calf where they belonged. I pocketed the lead again. Now, onto the books. My favorite human invention in the whole world (besides the internet, of course, but I hardly, if ever, had access to that).There was one in particular above the hundreds I’d stared at with knowledge-hungry eyes day after day, that I just had to crack open: Federal Statutes Protecting Domesticated and Captive Animals. The irony of the fact that I could read the contents within with a sharp, legal expertise did seem to expose the holes in human thinking when it came to classifying people like me as animals. But, in any case, these were the laws currently on the books and I wanted to garner as much information about them as I possibly could. I knew not every library would happen to have a book this specific to my interests. I just wished I was big enough to pocket it and take it with me. I made my way to the left side of the massive wall of shelves, hopping up on the ladder. Still hesitant to use my right hand, I clamored up to the 6th shelf, and found myself standing before the small, sea green volume with navy blue lettering on the spine. Boring, ordinary, yet containing a codified list of every legal precedent to date relating to people unlucky enough to be born like me. It was squeezed tightly between other, thicker volumes. I wrapped my fingers around its spine and pulled as hard as I could. Not only did it not budge, but I found myself teetering dangerously close to the edge of the shelf. Well, trying it like this is just going to get me killed. I slung the bag off of my shoulder, tossing it up and over the books to be grabbed in a moment, and hopped up, on top of the row of volumes, finding just enough space to squeeze between the top of the books below me and the next shelf above. Bracing myself against the back corner where the shelf and back wall met, I started to kick the volume forward, until I’d dislodged it enough to drop down and slip behind it. Using my shoulder as a battering ram, I shoved my full weight, which I knew was embarrassingly meager, into the book. Bit by bit it began to dislodge, getting closer and closer to the edge. The thickness of the book was just about equal to the width of my body, spine to sternum. So I was able to slide along with it. Just a few more shoves… and it’d go cascading to the ground. One. Two. Three. Light suddenly flooded my eyes as the book fell away, tumbling down, down, until, crash a flurry of cardboard backing and paper colliding with the floor. A smile brightened my face. I’d done it! My victory was extremely short lived, however, because as soon as the book landed on the hardwood floor, the heavy clunk of a brass doorknob sliding and the creak of wood on hinges rung in my ears. Someone was here!
